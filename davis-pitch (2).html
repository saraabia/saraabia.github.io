    Scope & purpose

    This tool produces a directional pre-feasibility read, not a substitute for a full architectural and financial feasibility study. The output is designed to sit at the top of a Davis-style deliverable and answer one question: is this site worth pursuing for the proposed use, at this scale?

    Land-value heuristic

    Per-m² land-value figures (landBase) are anchored on a synthesis of Mubawab and Avito listing prices, weighted toward closed-transaction-equivalents where the listing has been delisted within the last 60 days (a rough proxy for transaction). The ±12–18% band reflects observed listing-to-sale spread by neighborhood. Hivernage carries the widest band because of the higher proportion of one-off luxury transactions that distort medians.

    ROI calculation

    ROI = (GDV − total cost) / total cost over a 3-year build-and-sell cycle. The model assumes:

    • Buildable area = lot × 2.0 (typical Marrakech multi-story ratio; varies by zone and FAR)
    • Land cost = neighborhood landBase × lot size
    • Build cost = user-input €/m² × buildable area
    • Soft cost = 18% of (land + build) — covers permitting, design, financing carry, contingency
    • GDV = exit price/m² × buildable area × 0.85 (15% loss to common areas, circulation)
    • Exit price/m² = build cost × use-type multiplier (residential 2.8×, hospitality 3.4×, mixed-use 3.1×)
    • The reported band reflects ±4 percentage points around the center ROI to surface sensitivity

    Risk scoring

    Each of 5 dimensions (permit timeline, market liquidity, regulatory complexity, infrastructure access, macro/demand) is scored low / med / high per neighborhood based on the assumption set below. The overall risk verdict is determined by:

    2+ HIGH flagsHigh

    OtherwiseLow

    Sources

    • Listing prices: Mubawab.ma, Avito.ma — sampled and aggregated monthly
    • Permit timelines: OECD Building Permit Process reports + Banque al-Maghrib regional housing data
    • Macro signals: Bank Al-Maghrib housing index, World Cup 2030 host-city investment announcements, IMF/OECD MENA outlook
    • Comparable transactions: Synthesized from public listings and broker-disclosed transactions; figures are directional

    Known limitations

    • Only 5 Marrakech neighborhoods are currently modeled. Casablanca, Rabat, Tangier, and Paris all need their own datasets.
    • Use multipliers are static; in production these should adjust to current absorption rates and inventory.
    • No floor-area-ratio (FAR / coefficient d'occupation des sols) constraint logic yet — the model assumes a typical buildable ratio per zone.
    • Sensitivity table assumes linear cost-and-price elasticity, which breaks down at the extremes.
