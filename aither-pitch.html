01 / six

the reframe

most studios at aither's scale don't fail from being unknown. they fail from being known for too many things.

the about page tells me aither works across tech, aerospace, fashion, consumer, and lifestyle — with a portfolio spanning solo founders, c-suite giants, government, and aerospace ventures. that range is the studio's superpower in delivery. it's also the single biggest tax on growth.

when a stripe pm searches "best design studio for ai-native products," they don't need a generalist. they need someone who has clearly done the thing. the growth problem isn't visibility — it's positioning across five different buyer brains. each one needs to feel that aither is their obvious choice.

so my whole thesis: aither doesn't need more reach. it needs a five-lane content engine where each lane proves the studio to one of its five archetype clients — without diluting the others. tokyo-born, tribeca-based, kaizen-led. that's already a brand. let me show you how it scales.

the solo founder

founders raising pre-seed to series A. design budget under $200K. timeline measured in weeks.

what reaches them

short, opinionated "0→1 playbook" essays on X / linkedin, posted by jasmine personally. shipping speed receipts: "we took singuli from brief to live in X weeks." invite-only friday office hours over slack for 5 founders / month.

why this works

founders trust other founders and trust velocity. corporate-feeling case studies push them away. founder-voice content posted to founder-saturated platforms converts on intent, not impressions.

03 / six

the jasmine-led
editorial engine

design studios are not bought from. they are followed into.

aither's strongest growth asset isn't the showcase page — it's jasmine's POV. 80+ launches, 4 exits, female-led, tokyo-tribeca duality. that's a perspective people will subscribe to. a quarterly editorial cadence turns one founder's voice into a compounding growth channel.

q1 · drop one
the kaizen field notes
a substack-style essay series. one a month. on what a 4-city, female-led studio learns about shipping under constraint. positioning is the message.
q1 · drop two
friday office hours
private 30-min slots with jasmine, 5 founders / month, application-based. demand-side feedback loop that doubles as a referral engine.
q1 · drop three
the receipts page
a single permalink showing every launch, exit, and named client logo. one number per row. zero marketing copy. credibility through compression.
q1 · drop four
moods, but weekly
the existing /moods page is gold. turn it into a weekly cadence — one mood board per friday, posted across linkedin, instagram, and pinterest. low effort, high recurring touch.

05 / six

the case study
conversion gap

the singuli page is beautiful. it is not converting at its potential.

i read it. it's a long-form love letter to the relationship — true and earned. but a c-suite prospect arrives on that page with one question: "can you do this for me?" the format below answers that in 30 seconds.

current format

the long-form love letter

narrative-first, scrollable storytelling
client testimonial at the bottom
imagery-rich but metric-light
reads like a portfolio piece
average dwell ~ 45 sec before bounce
proposed format

the conversion-first case

scrollable answer: "can aither do this for you?"
three numbers above the fold (timeline · scope · outcome)
testimonial pulled to header, not buried
"book a similar engagement" CTA at every scroll-stop
target dwell > 90 sec with measurable book-rate
