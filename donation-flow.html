[04] / 2024 VS 2023 YEAR OVER YEAR

What changed from last year.

WCK grew significantly in 2024, driven by responses in Gaza, Ukraine, Spain (DANA flooding), and Morocco. Spending grew, but the program-to-overhead ratio actually improved.

[KEY INSIGHT]

WCK is one of the rare relief organizations where the largest single line item is literally food. Most large humanitarian groups spend more on logistics, salaries, or grants than on the relief item itself. Here, ingredients make up 60.5% of every dollar spent, the most direct ratio I've found in any major US relief 990 from 2024.

[05] / METHODOLOGY HOW THIS WAS BUILT

How this was actually built.

All numbers come from WCK's 2024 audited consolidated financial statements, prepared by Grant Thornton LLP (Arlington, VA), released on September 25, 2025. I parsed the audited Statement of Functional Expenses, which separates spending into the three IRS-required functional categories (programs, management & general, fundraising), and then drilled into the natural-expense classification within Programs (food, freight, salaries, etc.) to build the second-level breakdown.

The meals-per-dollar ratio in the calculator is calculated as WCK's reported 109+ million meals served in 2024 ÷ $281,892,063 in program expenses ≈ 0.387 meals per program dollar. This is a directional figure based on the organization's own reporting, not a unit-cost claim.

I built the dashboard in HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. No external libraries, no chart packages. The Sankey-style flow is hand-drawn SVG sized to actual proportions of the 2024 expense data. The donation calculator updates in real time as you move the slider, with bar fills animating via CSS transitions.
